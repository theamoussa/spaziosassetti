Quattrocento Carpetbagger, Sedrick Chisom's presentation of works created during his residency, opens on Saturday, March 29. Join us from 6–8 PM for our inaugural exhibition. The show will remain on view through May 20. To book your visit, please email spaziosassetti@gmail.com.

The freedom to integrate new aesthetic concerns is sustained by the artist’s distinct attention to surfaces; Sedrick Chisom remains fixated on opaque applications of paint and a consistent sensibility that allows for the discovery of new material languages. In Florence he explores the frescoes of San Marco, leafs through books on Giotto, and collects tabloids, aligning them all on the same plane of consideration. Here, fashion, religious iconography, and trash are incorporated into experimental painting-collages. Like a mutation of certain moves the artist has always made, these works speak to Chisom’s investment in his discrete visual landscape, while opening up to new encounters with the Italian city’s frequencies.


Sedrick Chisom (b. 1989, Philadelphia, PA) leans into his own myth-making and historically-bound proclivities. As such, his paintings are caught between a recuperated past and apocalyptic future, all of which is grounded in a stage play written by the artist. He habitually borrows from this plot in order to arrive at images that are both haunted and mystic in nature. Chisom lives and works in London.


Recent solo exhibitions have been held at Pilar Corrias, London (2024); CLEARING, New York (2024); Pilar Corrias, London (2021); and Matthew Brown, Los Angeles (2019). Group exhibitions include Frye Art Museum, Seattle (2022); Hayward Gallery, London (2022) and Kunsthal Rotterdam (2022–2023); and Possédées, MO.CO. Montpellier (2020).

His work is included in the collections of the Studio Museum in Harlem, New York; Aïshti Foundation, Beirut; Stedelijk Museum, Amsterdam; The Hessel Collection, Bard College, Annandale-on-Hudson; Elie Khouri Art Foundation, Dubai; Yuz Museum, Shanghai; George Economou Collection, Marousi; Igal Ahouvi Art Collection, Tel Aviv; Institute of Contemporary Art, Miami; KADIST, Paris; Longlati, Shanghai; May and Samuel Rudin Family Foundation, New York; Musée d’Art Moderne de Paris; and X Museum, Beijing.
